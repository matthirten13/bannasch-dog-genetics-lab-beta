I am a DVM/PhD Student part of the [Veterinary Scientist Training Program](https://vstp.vetmed.ucdavis.edu/program-overview) and [Integrative Genetics and Genomics](https://www.ucdavis.edu/graduate-programs/integrative-genetics-and-genomics) graduate group at UC Davis. My PhD research investigates LINE-1 (Long Interspersed Nuclear Element 1) retrotransposons in dogs and their contribution to cancer. Dogs are a powerful comparative model: they share our environment, develop spontaneous cancers with biology that mirrors human disease, and their breed structure makes genetic discovery tractable. My work aims to characterize canine LINE-1 biology with translational implications for both veterinary and human medicine by evaluating methylation, genetic variants, and transcriptional activity using a bioinformatics based approach.
