My PhD research investigates LINE-1 (Long Interspersed Nuclear Element 1) retrotransposons in dogs and their contribution to cancer. LINE-1s are the only elements in the mammalian genome capable of auto-retrotransposition, the process of copying and pasting themselves elsewhere in the genome. LINE-1 elements comprise roughly 16% of mammalian genomes (far more than the ~1– 2% that codes for proteins) and have been increasingly implicated in human cancer, cardiovascular disease, and neurodegeneration, yet their biology in dogs is largely uncharacterized. Dogs are a powerful comparative model: they share our environment, develop spontaneous cancers with biology that mirrors human disease, and their breed structure makes genetic discovery tractable. My work aims to characterize canine LINE-1 biology with translational implications for both veterinary and human medicine by evaluating methylation, genetic variants, and transcriptional activity using a bioinformatics based approach.
